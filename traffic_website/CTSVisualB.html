City Regions with Cameras Marked

This map displays the traffic regions we used in our analysis. The regions were defined by the city of Chicago in order to aid in providing estimates of current congestion on the city streets. Separating the city into different regions allowed us to assess whether the amount of traffic cameras in a certain region correlated with the number or severity of accidents in that region.

There are two types of traffic cameras employed by the city of Chicago: red light cameras and speed cameras. They are marked with different colors in the graphic. In the city of Chicago, speed cameras are found only in areas adjacent to a school or a park, and are intended to decrease the fatality rate of accidents, specifically those involving children.
